Common Questions

Answers to what most clients ask before engaging us

We have handled these questions enough to know exactly what concerns clients most before they reach out. Here are direct answers.

How long does business registration actually take?
Business name registrations are completed in 3 to 5 working days. Company incorporations take 5 to 7 working days. All required documentation must be provided upfront. We keep you updated at every stage of the process.
Do I need a lawyer to register my business in Nigeria?
You are not legally required to, but working with experienced practitioners protects you from structural mistakes, ensures full compliance, and provides the right documentation for banking, funding, and contracts from day one.
What documents will I need to get started?
Requirements vary by entity type but typically include valid means of identification, a proposed business name, a registered address, and details of the founders or directors. We will provide a precise checklist when you book a consultation.
Can foreign nationals incorporate a company in Nigeria?
Yes. Foreign nationals and companies can incorporate in Nigeria under CAMA 2020. We advise on the best structure, assist with CAC registration, and where applicable handle business permit and expatriate quota applications.
How are your fees structured?
Our fees are transparent and all-inclusive, covering professional fees, CAC statutory charges, and applicable stamp duties. We discuss pricing clearly at the consultation stage before any engagement begins. There are no hidden costs.
